## How to read this

Each piece has two cue lines:

- **LX** (lighting) — the stage look. Trigger on the operator's **GO** unless noted.
  Looks are suggestions tuned to each piece's mood; hand this to the LX op/venue tech
  as a starting point and adjust to the rig you actually have.
- **VIDEO** — the projector. In this show the **ShowRunner app drives the projector**:
  pressing **GO** fades the piece's **title card** up over 1 s; **Esc/STOP** fades to
  black. So most VIDEO cues are just "GO → title card; STOP → black at applause."
  `_Show Assets/Black.png` is the explicit "projector off / black" slide if you need it.

**Global conventions**
- **Pre-show / house:** house lights up, projector black (or a holding/welcome slide).
- **Between pieces:** dim to a low blue/neutral transition wash; projector to black
  (press **Esc/STOP** after applause) so the next title card lands clean on GO.
- **EDM pieces (7, 8, 9, 10, E3):** the app also starts backing + click on GO — cue
  bigger, more saturated, moving looks here; the four-in-a-row block (7–10) is the
  show's high-energy centerpiece.
- **TRIO / DUET:** widen the look to cover the extra players; add a second special for
  the violin (and cello).
- **PANIC:** Esc kills audio and fades the projector to black instantly — if that fires
  unexpectedly, LX should snap to a neutral wash and hold.

## Main Program

### 1 — Fantaisie-Impromptu  *(solo · opener)*
- **LX:** Open from house-to-half into a cool blue/white **piano special**; gentle build
  through the agitato outer sections, warm/soften for the lyrical middle, lift again to
  end. This is the "lights come down, show begins" moment.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card up. Hold for the piece. STOP → black at applause.

### 2 — Rachmaninoff Prelude in G minor  *(solo · Op. 23 No. 5)*
- **LX:** March-like and dramatic — deeper amber/red wash with a strong front special;
  pull back to intimate for the lyrical central section, then full and bold for the return.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### 3 — Rolling Thunder  *(solo · original · NEW)*
- **LX:** Stormy. Cold steel-blue/white base with darker edges; sharp **white accent
  flashes** on the biggest hits (a restrained "lightning" — avoid full strobe unless
  the venue/audience is fine with it). Builds in intensity to the climax.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card *(needs new TitleCard.png — see Assets)*. STOP → black.

### 4 — Fires of a Revolution  *(solo · original · NEW)*
- **LX:** Fiery and defiant — saturated reds/oranges with amber, flickering warm
  texture if available; drive harder into the climax. Hottest look of the first half.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card *(needs new TitleCard.png — see Assets)*. STOP → black.

### 5 — Colors of the Soul  *(solo · original)*
- **LX:** Let the title lead — a **slow color-shifting wash** (blue → violet → warm →
  green) tracking the emotional arc; keep a steady piano special underneath so the face
  stays lit while colors move around it.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### 6 — Fight for Freedom  *(solo · original)*
- **LX:** Anthemic — bold white/gold front light, broad and rising; biggest, brightest
  solo look of the set for the finish. *(Piece is "maybe" — keep a cut option ready.)*
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### — 🎤 Talk → intro the EDM set —
- **LX:** Up to a comfortable speaking wash on Lionel. **VIDEO:** STOP to black (or a
  holding slide) while he talks.

### 7 — Torrent Etude Nightmare  *(EDM · backing + click)*
- **LX:** Drop into the club look — dark, saturated, **moving lights / color chase** with
  beat-driven energy; this kicks off the four-piece EDM block. Keep a piano special so
  the playing still reads.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card + backing/click start. STOP → black.

### 8 — Für Elise Nightmare  *(EDM)*
- **LX:** Dubstep-purple — magenta/violet with hard white strobes/stabs on the drops;
  punchiest, most aggressive look of the block.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card + audio. STOP → black.

### 9 — Canon in Dream  *(EDM)*
- **LX:** Dreamier within the block — cyan/violet, slow sweeps and soft pulsing rather
  than hard strobe; ethereal.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card + audio. STOP → black.

### 10 — Moonlight Sonata Nightmare  *(EDM)*
- **LX:** Start in still **moonlight blue** for the famous opening, then break into the
  full saturated club look on the drop — biggest moment of the EDM block before the talk.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card + audio. STOP → black.

### — 🎤 Talk —
- **LX:** Speaking wash. **VIDEO:** black / holding slide.

### 11 — Dreams of a Violin  *(DUET · vln + pno)*
- **LX:** Warm and intimate — two soft specials (violin + piano), gentle amber wash;
  romantic, low-key after the EDM block.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### 12 — Gallop  *(TRIO · vln + vc)*
- **LX:** Bright, lively, warm full-stage cover for all three players; energetic and open.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### 13 — Beethoven Virus  *(TRIO · vln + vc · main-set closer)*
- **LX:** High-energy finish — bold red/white, full and driving, biggest trio look; build
  to a strong button for the end of the main set.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### — 🎤 Closing remarks —
- **LX:** Speaking wash, then dim toward applause/exit. **VIDEO:** black / holding slide.

## Encores

### E1 — Sunflowers  *(solo · original)*
- **LX:** Warm golden/yellow wash, soft and generous — the "encore glow."
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### E2 — Flight of the Bumblebee  *(solo · showpiece)*
- **LX:** Bright, playful, quick — crisp white/amber, maybe a light fast color flutter to
  match the speed. Short and dazzling.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card. STOP → black.

### E3 — Still D.R.E.  *(EDM · closer)*
- **LX:** Biggest look of the night — full club/party state, color chase, audience
  light/wash if available; send them out on a high.
- **VIDEO:** GO → title card + audio. STOP → black, then bring **house up** for bows/exit.
